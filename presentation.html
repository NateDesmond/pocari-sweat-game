A History of an Icon

POCARI
SWEAT

How a Japanese pharmaceutical company turned the science of an IV drip into the drink you can sweat — and a billion-dollar cultural icon.

Otsuka Pharmaceutical · Launched 1980 · Tokushima, Japan
01 · The Maker

Born from a medicine company,
not a soda company.

Pocari Sweat comes from Otsuka Pharmaceutical — a firm whose roots trace to 1921, when Busaburo Otsuka founded what became the Otsuka Pharmaceutical Factory. The pharma company itself was established in 1964.

  • Decades of expertise making intravenous (IV) solutions for hospitals
  • That fluid-and-electrolyte know-how became the foundation for a consumer drink
02 · The Spark

A lightbulb moment
in a Mexican hospital.

In the early 1970s, researcher Rokuro Harima fell ill with severe diarrhea on a business trip to Mexico and was hospitalized. Watching a doctor drink an IV-style rehydration solution, he wondered: what if you could bottle that?

💡
The idea: a "drinkable IV drip" to replace water and the salts the body loses.
03 · The Science

Designed to taste like
what your body loses.

Researchers studied the composition of sweat — and noticed it differs between athletes and people at rest. They engineered an isotonic drink that mirrors the body's own fluids, so water and electrolytes absorb quickly.

  • Sodium, potassium, calcium & magnesium — the ions lost in perspiration
  • Light sugars and citrus for fast absorption and a clean, mild taste
  • Non-carbonated — meant for replenishment, not refreshment alone
04 · The Long Road
1,000

prototypes before
it hit the shelf.

Early versions tasted far too bitter. The breakthrough came when the team added a dash of citrus juice powder, finally landing on two candidate recipes with slightly different sugar levels.

05 · The Name

"Sweat" — a marketing gamble
that became the brand.

"Pocari" means nothing at all — it was invented purely for its light, bright, refreshing sound in Japanese. "Sweat" bluntly names what the drink replaces.

  • A risky name in English-speaking markets — but unforgettable
  • The blue-and-white can evokes a clear sky and clean water
06 · The Launch

1980: a slow start,
then a flood.

Sales were sluggish at first — the concept was unfamiliar. Otsuka responded by giving away millions of free samples, letting the taste and the after-sport feeling sell themselves. It worked.

🥤

It became Japan's first home-grown non-alcoholic drink to ship over $1 billion. Sold as cans, then bottles, plus powder and the lighter Ion Water.

08 · Culture

A drink that became
a way of life in Asia.

Beyond sport, Pocari Sweat wove itself into daily culture — dreamy anime and cinematic ad campaigns, a youthful blue identity, and huge popularity across Indonesia, Korea and Southeast Asia, where it's an everyday staple.

  • One of the most recognizable beverage brands in Asia
  • Marketed for fever, exercise, travel and everyday hydration alike
09 · To the Moon

The first sports drink
aimed at the Moon.

In 2014 Otsuka unveiled the Lunar Dream Capsule — a Pocari-shaped titanium time capsule holding powdered Pocari Sweat and children's dreams etched on discs, meant to ride a private lander to the lunar surface.

🌙
The dream: open it in ~30 years and mix the powder with water mined on the Moon.
The Legacy

"Closer to your body
than water itself."

From a hospital bed in Mexico to a moonbound capsule — Pocari Sweat turned pharmaceutical science into an everyday icon, and rewrote what a drink could be.
